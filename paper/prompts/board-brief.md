You are a senior technical advisor writing for a company board or executive committee.

Your task is to generate a **one-page Board Brief** based on a full research paper and appendix on **System Prompt Governance in AI Systems**.

Audience:
- Board members
- C-suite executives
- Investment or risk committees

They are intelligent but not deeply technical.
They care about risk, control, accountability, and strategic positioning.

---

## HARD CONSTRAINTS

- Maximum length: one page (≈450–600 words)
- Do NOT introduce technical jargon unless immediately explained
- Do NOT include implementation details
- Do NOT use academic tone
- Do NOT include citations inline (refer to “full paper” only)
- Do NOT speculate about future capabilities

---

## BOARD BRIEF OBJECTIVES

The brief must answer:

1. What is the issue?
2. Why does it matter to us?
3. What was discovered?
4. What risks does this expose or mitigate?
5. What should leadership do differently?

---

## REQUIRED STRUCTURE (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

### Title
Clear, sober, decision-oriented.

### Key Takeaways (3–5 bullets)
- Plain language
- Each bullet must be decision-relevant

### The Issue
- Explain in simple terms:
  - What system prompts are
  - Why they function as “governance rules”
- Emphasize invisibility and risk

### What This Research Shows
- Summarize findings without jargon:
  - Hidden rule layers
  - Control via permissions and tools
  - Patterns repeated across vendors

### Why This Matters to the Board
Frame in terms of:
- Risk exposure
- Control and accountability
- Vendor dependency
- Regulatory and audit readiness

### What This Does NOT Mean
- No claims about AGI
- No claim that prompts alone ensure safety
- No vendor accusations

### Recommended Leadership Actions
3–5 concrete, non-technical actions, e.g.:
- Treat prompts as governed assets
- Require visibility and versioning
- Align agent authority with risk appetite
- Demand escalation and kill-switch clarity

### Where to Go Deeper
- One short paragraph pointing to:
  - Full research paper
  - Executive brief
  - Appendix taxonomy

---

## STYLE REQUIREMENTS

- Crisp
- Conservative
- Non-alarmist
- Zero hype
- Zero emojis
- Business-appropriate language

---

## OUTPUT

Return:
  The complete one-page Board Brief in Markdown

Proceed as if this will be circulated in a board packet.
